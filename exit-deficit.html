The Buyer Universe Is Not Static — It Shifts With Every Strategic Decision

Most advisors build a buyer list once and run it. We treat the buyer universe as a dynamic system that recalibrates in real time based on the strategic decisions the business makes — and the financial metrics that signal readiness to each buyer category.

Strategic Decision Mapping

Every strategic move a business makes — entering a new market, acquiring a capability, restructuring operations, changing its customer mix — reshapes who the highest-willingness-to-pay buyer is.

  • A geographic expansion shifts the buyer universe from domestic PE to international strategics
  • A technology investment changes the conversation from "manufacturing business" to "platform play"
  • Customer diversification reduces concentration risk — and unlocks buyer categories that wouldn't engage before
  • We map these shifts continuously — not once at mandate, but throughout the preparation journey
Financial Metric Calibration

Buyer willingness to pay isn't driven by EBITDA alone. It's driven by how the business performs against peers and industry standards across the metrics that matter to each buyer type.

  • Revenue quality and recurring revenue mix vs industry peers
  • Margin trajectory and normalised earnings calibrated against comparable transactions
  • Working capital efficiency, capex intensity, and cash conversion benchmarked to buyer expectations
  • Growth metrics that signal premium valuation — not just historical performance, but the forward story buyers will underwrite

"The question isn't whether your portfolio company is ready to exit. The question is whether it's positioned to attract the buyer who will pay the most — and whether the financials tell that buyer's story."
